West Coast, CA 67° May 16, 2022

A Day Exploring the West Coast

Fair warning, there will be a LOT of images and videos from today. We packed in a full day of redwood trees, ocean views, rock climbing, and scenic drives.

The day began with a short but scenic drive from Giles studio to Henry Cowell Redoods State Park where we purchased a park pass and parked to begin hiking. I have been dying to return to the Redwood trees ever since my 10-year old trip with my dad when we visited the Redwood National Park in Northern California. The trees are incredible how tall they reach into the sky and the bark is so tough it almost appears as a kind of polymer or plastic. We didn't take a long hike through the park but we saw many incredible trees, a small stream that was kind to rock skipping, and tons of birds and other wildlife. I love to hike and explore nature and the Redwood forests have always fascinated me, I could have spent all day just wandering the trails and marvelling at the trees. Some of my favorite pictures we took are here below, the scale of the tree against me and Sarah is just incredible.

Once we left the Redwood Park we took another scenic drive down to Santa Cruz to see some seals and walk the wharf. Santa Cruz was extremelly active, with people walking the beach, sailing in the bay, and fishing off the wharf. By far the coolest part of Santa Cruz was the countless seals swimming in the water and terrorizing the birds. Looking into the water you could see seals flipping through the water as they navigated around each other and to the surface to scare unsuspecting birds floating in the water. At the far end of the wharf there was openings in the walkway where we could look down and see the seals sunbathing and resting on the wooden beams. There was hardly enough room for the seals and we saw several try to jump up, only to knock off some of their friends. Before leaving we stopped in one of the sweets stores on the wharf and purchased some chocolate and maple candy. You can see my reaction to my first bite which I hope to never forget on the right.

The stop at Santa Cruz was short and we were getting hungry so we made our way to Pebble Beach to have lunch. We drove along 17 mile drive to pebble beach which had incredible views over Monterey Bay and made two stops prior to lunch. The first stop was a bathroom break that was really just an excuse to see the luxury of 5-star resort The Inn at Spanish Bay and the second stop was to take some pictures of The Lone Tree seen on the right and take some views of the bay.

We had lunch at Stillwater Bar & Grill located at Pebble Beach Resorts. I got a delicious personal pizza and we had a nice lunch outside by a gas fire while we could look out onto the bay and watch golfers at the 18th hole. This was a great way to enjoy the views and relax after our busy morning and we enjoyed the break. Giles was bragging about how he had gotten the trip down to a science and that we were just on schedule. I would love to come back sometime during whale watching season and try to spot some whales out in the bay. It was slightly overcast which was a little dissapointing, however it made it a little more comfortable to sit oustide without the glare of the sun. Perhaps some day in the future I will be deceptive enough to trick some golfers into thinking I am capable of these courses, I can only image how cool it must be to have these views while carting around a golf course of that caliber.

After stopping for lunch we started our drive on Hwy 1 famous for its incredible views of the west coast. The highway definitely lived up to its reputation and I spent the entire drive looking out the windows at the rolling hills and infinitely spanning ocean landscape. We stopped right before the bridge pictures on the right to take some pictures and walk around.

We also stopped briefly at a park to do a quick nature walk along the coast and run on some rocks, however they were closing soon so we went to another public beach access off of Hwy 1 to get closer to the water and do some more climbing. It turned out that this beach was entirely empty and we found a little cove to climb rocks and take some pictures of the beautiful ocean. There is way too many to include just 1 so I put them in a gallery below.

Our day of exploration ended in Carmel to watch the sunset and walk through the beautiful beach-side town. Halfway through the walk we had all decided this was where we would retire; the houses were beautiful with perfect views of the sunset and Monterey bay. Surfers and swimmers were everywhere on the beach and the town center had numerous small restaurants and storefronts that looked as if they were straight out of a hallmark movie. We found a good spot to watch the sunset and relaxed as our day of adventure came to close.

To fully end the day, we stopped at In-and-Out for a burger on the way home and ate it while watching a couple episodes of How I Met Your Mother before turning in. Hard to image any way today could have gone any better.
