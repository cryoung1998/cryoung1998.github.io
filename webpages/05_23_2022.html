Kailua, HI 85° May 23, 2022

Riding the bus

Today started out pretty quiet as me and Sarah completed some research on transportation and looked to get two bikes we had found on Facebook marketplace. We found out that the bus system is really well developed here and affordable, only requiring a $2 permanent bus card that can be preloaded with money and maxing out at $5.50/day in riding fares. We came up with a plan to explore the local area bus routes as we went to get our bikes, but first we had a nice lunch on the porch with some fresh pineapple we had bought at the local grocery store. Luckily as we were going to leave into town Greg offerd us a ride, so we got the opportunity to ride in his VW Eos which is a convertible. It was really nice to enjoy the weather driving into town.

Once we got to town we picked up our bus passes and loaded some money on them before ubering back to our house. We wanted to regroup before setting back off, and once we had checked out the bus schedule we set off for the closest stop. When we got on the bus the driver immediately knew we were unfamiliar and allowed us to stand at the front and ask some questions about where we were going. He gave us a great tip to use google maps because it will automatically plan out bus stops and routes. Using his advice we mapped out a route to the bike I was hoping to buy in Kaneohe, the town just north of Kailua.

The bus ride around towards Kaneohe was extremelly pretty and it was cool to see some of the residential areas by the beach. Once we got to my bike we were a little dissapointed to find out that the bike tire was wobbly and the brakes needed to be tightened a ton. Not to mention that the gear shifter didn't work. Needless to say we didn't pick that bike up and took the bus back towards town to get Sarah's bike. That bike was way nicer, even including a bottle opener on the frame that I highly doubt will ever get any use. We picked up the bike and walked back into town to catch one more bus back to our neighborhood. On the way back we saw a chicken make a dart accross the road to avoid being run over by a car. Sarah, seeming to have prepared for this moment her whole life, was overjoyed to ask, "Why did the chicken cross the road?".

Lucky the bus stops just outside our neighborhood ~7 minutes from our house so it was easy to walk our new bike and beach chair purchase from target back home, marking a successful day around town.
