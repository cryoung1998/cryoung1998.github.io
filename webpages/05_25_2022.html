Kailua, HI 85° May 25, 2022

First Beach Day

Today was Sarah and my first real day at the beach in town. We took the bus in the morning to the closest stop by the beach and walked the remaining 5 minutes to Kailua Beach Park. This has to be one of the prettiest beaches I have ever been to and it was incredibly relaxing to sit by the water and enjoy the scenery. There is a lake in the neighborhood we live in known as "Enchanted Lake" that connects via a small canal to the ocean during high tides, however when we were there it was seperated by a small patch of sand where people were playing volleyball. The water was perfectly refreshing, not cold but definitely not the uncomfortably warm water familiar to South Carolinians in late summer.

For the first hour or so I read The Subtle Knife, the second book in Phillip Pullmans His Dark Materials trilogy. It was fun to watch the Hawaiin Outrigger canoes paddle along the shore and see so many families playing in the park. It is clear that Kailua remains a largely local beach in comparison to the busy shores on the east side of the island. We did see several visitors like us, but the large majority of beach goers had an "Aloha" auro about them that seems to be inherent to the locals. After relaxing for a while, Sarah and I took a swim and then batted around a wiffle ball with some paddles we found at Target. This was suprisingly fun considering we were aimlessly wacking a ball back and forth.

Once we got home we finally cut upon the papayas our neighbors had so generously given us. I had no idea how horrible the inside of a papaya smells... it was awful. Once we had properly disposed off the terrible smelling seeds and thoroughly cleaned the kitchen we actually got to enjoy the fruit. I have to say that it was an interesting flavor, still not decided on whether I liked it or not but safe to say it's unlike any other fruit I have ever had.

Overall it was an extremelly successful first beach day and I can't wait to spend more time here in the beautiful waters!
